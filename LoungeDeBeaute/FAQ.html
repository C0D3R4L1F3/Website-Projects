Microblading

What is Microblading?

Phibrows Microblading is a manual "hand" method of permanent cosmetics for your eyebrows which creates extremely fine natural looking hair strokes. The results? Perfectly natural looking, thick, full brows.

Micro-shading

What is Micro-shading?

This is a technique done by machine and with a manual pen. It is 2 techniques in 1 session. This is great if you are looking for a a solid fill look with some natural hair strokes peeking through. Most clients would have the hair strokes in the front and the rest filled. It creates the light to dark brow effect.

Ombre/Powder Brows

What is Ombre/Powder Brows?

This is a technique done by machine and it is a solid fill brow. Great if you have existing tattoos or just love the look of filled in brows.

Stages of Microblading

AFTERCARE & MAXIMIZING YOUR RESULTS

After treatment no makeup, foundation or any other creams over the eye brows except the one your artist recommend you. On the first day of your treatment; you may experience some swelling and a mild headache after the procedure. Eyebrows must be rinse out every 3-4 hours and cream must be applied throughout the day if the procedure was done in the morning. If you had the procedure done in the evening, please make sure to check that there is enough cream before bed. Second day - Rinse the brows and reapply cream every 3-4 hours. (4x in total for the day) On the third day to the seventh day after the treatment, cream must be applied to the eyebrows several times a day; minimum 2 times. Do not rinse with water or get it wet in any way until your brows are fully healed. 7-10 days. Some may take longer to heal, so keep reapplying cream until you are fully healed and all scabs have flaked off on its own. After 7 days you should avoid any increase sweating, sports, sauna, tanning salon, laser, sun and chemical treatments or performing tasks related to cleaning the house, etc. Please also avoid eating spicy food, smoking and various drinks can lead to slow healing of the wounds. If the consumption of the aforementioned substances is not reduced it can lead to hygiene risks increase. If you experience excessive swelling or an allergic reaction, you should consult your doctor. If an allergic reaction causes difficulty in breathing or swallowing, call 911 or go to the emergency room immediately. This is very rare but can happen.

PREVENTING INFECTION

Always use a clean Q-tip to apply the ointment and reverse the Q-tip for the other brows

Avoid touching the area with your hands and make sure hair is pulled away from the face

Absolutely no makeup on the area where tattooed. This can lead to serious infection

Avoid any strenuous activity that causes excess sweating

Avoid sun exposure/tanning beds for up to 6 weeks to prevent premature fading

APPEARANCE

During the first 7 days, itching and flaking may appear. PLEASE DO NOT SCRATCH or pick at the wound or pull any flakes! You will pull away all the work that has been done.

The tattoo will also appear very dark and lines become 20% thicker. Your skin may appear swollen and you may have a slight headache. Please do not worry as this is normal. The color will fade 20-50% within a week or so. Any unexpected problems with the skin healing, please contact your artist.

⦁ Healing of deeper wounds last between 14 - 21 days, thus a touch up is needed for the final result.

⦁ It is recommended that you book your touch up within 5-6 weeks after your first session for maxim results.

Eyeliner & Lash Enhancement Aftercare

EYELINER & LASH ENHANCEMENT AFTERCARE

AThere are a few reactions that may occur such as; redness or a purple hue around the treated area which will subside in 24hrs. You may experience some swelling for 1-2 days which is normal. Use a cold pack of ice in the morning to reduce this. Sleep slightly elevated for the first night.

If your eyes remain swollen for more than 2 days or producing mucous, please seek a medical doctor.

What to expect:

  • Eyelash may stick together the next morning
  • Puffy eyelids for 2-3 days
  • Redness or mild bruising around the eyes
  • Itchiness (if poor hygiene is used, an infection can occur, please see a doctor)
  • Swelling and tenderness
  • *If swelling last beyond 2 days, remove all ointment and keep dry

    • Daily Care:

  • Apply ointment (Vaseline) many times a day for 3-5 days
  • Do not touch the treated area unless applying ointment
  • No scratching, rubbing or picking the treated area
  • Do not apply eye makeup around treated area until you are healed
  • If your lashes stick together, moisten with distilled water and remove residue
  • If you wear contact lenses, you may wear them 1 day after the procedure
  • Artificial tears may be used
  • Do not pick the remaining pigment out with a Q-tip.
  • Touch up may be done after 21 days
  • Discontinue the ointment if healed
  • You may now wear makeup. Always start with a new tube of mascara to avoid infection

    How often do I need a touch up?

    Touch ups are recommended every six months to a year, to keep the color fresh and the strokes crisp.

    Do I have to remove my eyebrow hair?

    We do not remove any hair when microblading. We only remove the hair surrounding the outside of your desired shape.

    Can makeup be worn on the brows while healing?

    bsolutely not. You will risk the chance of infection. You can start filling them in once you see all flakes has fallen off.

    What products do you use?

    All our products are High End, Vegan and cruelty free. Every needle is sterilized in its own package and thrown out after each use. We DO NOT use the same needle more then once.

    What should I do after the procedure?

    Instructions with your aftercare kit will be provided after the treatment. You just need to keep the area dry and clean. Try to avoid any activities that consist of water and sweating. If you lose the instructions, please visit the after care section.

    How long does it take to heal?

    Healing time varies for each individual . Usually, it takes about 7 to 10 days for the epithelial crust to form and slough off. Your skin will be considered healed after that happens. Then, the pigment takes some time to settle into the skin. A good month should pass before we consider the process complete.

    What if I don’t like it?

    A good technician always make sure that you are satisfied with the shape that is drawn on prior to the first appointment. If for some reason you change your mind and wish to change the shape, we can work with you within the possibilities to achieve it.

    Can microblading work for existing tattoo?

    In order to help you we must receive a clear photo of your brows to assess options. Results varies for each person depending on how the previous tattoo looks.

    What tattoo removal method do you use?

    A non surgical method with shorter healing time than Laser treatment.

    The tattoo removal tool is used in a similar way as tattooing or micropigmenting , with the same equipment. The tattoo removal formula is placed where the unwanted tattoo is and almost immediately draw the orginal ink upwards and out of the skin.

    After treatment:

    The scab with tattoo ink will be formed over the treated area in the next few days. While the skin heals, even more unwanted tattoo ink will be drawn out. The scab will start to peel off naturally, extracting the unwanted tattoo ink. You are likely to have a secondary scab containing even more tattoo ink. After the procedure, and as long as you have the scab on the skin, keep the treated area dry.

    Several treatments may be required to remove the unwanted tattoo. Average is between 2-6 pending on the severity of the tattoo. Consultation is required.
